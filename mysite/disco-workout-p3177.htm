</div><br style="clear:both;" /></div><br style="clear:both;" /><p><a href="http://www.discoworkout.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/nb2.jpg"><img src="http://www.discoworkout.com/wp-content/uploads/2012/07/nb2-300x225.jpg" alt="" title="nb2" width="300" height="225" class="aligncenter size-medium wp-image-3191" /></a><br />
I feel a bit guilty since I haven&#8217;t posted in a while, so I&#8217;ve recorded the three 45s that I picked up while on vacation in Austin (all three of these were picked up at Friends Of Sound Records, I highly recommend the place, and make sure to check their back room). Austin, being the live music capital of the world (is that true?), obviously has a great deal of hip music folks milling about, but since it is in Texas, it also has a great deal of country (dare I say &#8220;not hip&#8221;, no disrespect to country, but), backwoods/hick types. Now I have no problem with either creed, however, when these conditions are present, there are other sects which tend to form, generally hybrids of the aformentioned two, the &#8220;hipster hick&#8221; or &#8220;hickster&#8221; (has that term been coined yet? according to urbandictionary.com it <a href="http://www.urbandictionary.com/define.php?term=hickster">has</a> except it doesn&#8217;t mean what I want it too&#8230;see Kings of Leon), and the country-western homeboy&#8230;and they are everywhere in Austin (I wish I could say that I saw someone in Salvatore Ferragamo Loafers and an ICP t-shirt but it was not to be)! Look at me on my high horse judging you from San Francisco.</p>
